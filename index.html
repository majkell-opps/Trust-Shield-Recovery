    Cases that could have happened to you

    Transaction Details

  • Transaction History:
    If you made a transaction and the funds were sent to the wrong address or a wrong network, check your wallet's transaction history. You can use a blockchain explorer (like blockchair.com, blockchain.com/explorer) to verify whether your transaction went through and where it went.
  • Mistyped Address:
    If the funds were sent to an incorrect address, unfortunately, there is no way to reverse the transaction unless you control the address (e.g., if it was an address you own and made a typo).

    • Wrong Wallet

  • If you sent to the wrong address:
    Unfortunately, blockchain transactions are irreversible, and unless the person on the other end is willing to return the funds, there's not much you can do.
  • If you sent to the wrong network:
    For example, sending Ethereum (ETH) to an Ethereum Classic (ETC) wallet, or sending tokens to the wrong chain, you might be able to recover the funds depending on the wallet's capabilities. Some wallets allow you to recover funds sent to a wrong network, while others do not

    • Lost access to account

  • Check Email :
    Most exchanges offer a password recovery feature. If you've forgotten your password or can't log in, look for a "Forgot Password?" option on the exchange's login page. Follow the instructions and enter the email address you used to register the account. You'll receive a link to reset your password.
  • 2FA Authentication

    Use a backup code: Many exchanges provide backup codes when you enable 2FA, which you can use to regain access.
